About the Programs

In first year Media, Information, and Technoculture, students are required to take 1.5 courses from the Faculty of Information and Media Studies.

MIT 1020 - Introduction to Media, Information, and Technoculture provides students with an overview of media studies and critical culture studies. Students will learn about the political economy of media and cover topics like: The Male Gaze, The Panopticon, and The Medium is the Message. Students will be given the chance to practice their essay writing by bringing in real world modern examples and applying them to these topics outlined above.

MIT 1025 - First Year Foundations is a writing course that teaches students how to write essays - a skill many incoming students need. Basic grammar is often overlooked and this class reinforces the need for literacy. The course is designed to teach you how to be more argumentative in your essays helping you create stronger pieces of writing. This course is only offered in first semester so that students can hopefully improve on their writing in the other mandatory course by the end of the school year.
